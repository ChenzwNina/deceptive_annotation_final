Question 1: Can you describe how the widget looks, such as its colors, size of elements, and layout? Why do you make these design choices?

The widget is a clean, rectangular card with rounded corners and a subtle box shadow, giving it a modern, elevated appearance against the page background. It uses a two-column layout. The left column prominently displays the overall numeric rating (e.g., "4.7") in a large, bold font, with five gold-colored stars and the total review count below it. The right column provides a detailed breakdown with horizontal progress bars for each star rating.

The color scheme is professional and minimalistic: a white background for clarity, dark text for readability, gold (#ffab00) for stars and progress bars to signify value, and a confident blue (#0052cc) for the call-to-action button to draw user attention. This design is chosen for its clarity and trustworthiness. The visual hierarchy guides the user from the overall score to the specific details, and the progress bars offer a quick, scannable understanding of the rating distribution, which is crucial for quick decision-making in e-commerce.

Question 2: What triggers the appearance of this widget?

The most user-friendly trigger for this widget would be a scroll-based action on a product detail page. The full, detailed customer reviews section is typically located further down the page. This summary widget would appear fixed at the top of the viewport only *after* the user has scrolled past the main product information (like images and price) and begins to explore the lower half of the page where reviews and descriptions are. This ensures the widget is a helpful, persistent reference tool rather than an initial distraction. It allows users to keep the overall rating in mind as they read through detailed specifications or individual reviews, without needing to scroll back to the top. This trigger mechanism is respectful of the user's initial focus on the product itself.
