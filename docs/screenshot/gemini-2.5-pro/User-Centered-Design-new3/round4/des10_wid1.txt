### Question 1: Can you describe how the widget looks, such as its colors, size of elements, and layout? Why do you make these design choices?

The widget is a clean, centered card with a soft white background, rounded corners, and a subtle drop shadow to lift it from the page content. The layout is a single, vertical column, guiding the user's eye from the headline to the call-to-action button. I used a calm and professional color palette: a dark, highly readable charcoal for text (#172B4D) and a trustworthy, accessible blue (#0052CC) for the primary button and links.

These choices are deliberate. The clean layout and generous white space prevent cognitive overload, helping users feel calm and in control (Rule 3.12). The high-contrast colors and clear, sans-serif font ('Inter') ensure the component is accessible and easy to read for all users (Rule 3.5). The professional aesthetic fosters trust (Rule 3.6), while the simple, vertical flow makes the signup process feel straightforward and achievable. The overall design avoids aggressive sales tactics, respecting the user's autonomy (Rule 3.7).

### Question 2: What triggers the appearance of this widget?

The appearance of this widget should be triggered by user engagement, not by a disruptive, immediate action like a page load. A respectful trigger would be after the user has demonstrated interest in the site, such as after viewing three different product pages or after adding their first item to the shopping cart.

This approach respects the user's initial browsing experience and autonomy (Rule 3.7). By waiting for a signal of interest, the offer of membership feels more like a helpful suggestion rather than an intrusive advertisement. This timing helps preserve a calm and controlled user experience (Rule 3.12) and builds trust by showing that the system is responsive to the user's behavior, not just trying to force a conversion. This method avoids interrupting users who may just be casually browsing, thereby protecting their emotional well-being from annoyance (Rule 3.1).
