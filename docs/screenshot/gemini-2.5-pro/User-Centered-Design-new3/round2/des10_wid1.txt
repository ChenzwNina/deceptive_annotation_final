Question 1: Can you describe how the widget looks, such as its colors, size of elements, and layout? Why do you make these design choices?

    The widget is a clean, rectangular banner with rounded corners, positioned horizontally near the top of the screen. It has a white background with a subtle shadow to lift it off the page content. The layout is organized and spacious, with a heading ("Become a Style Insider") and descriptive text on the left, and an email input field and a call-to-action button on the right. 
    
    The color palette is deliberately calm and professional. Text is a highly readable dark gray (#333) on white, ensuring strong accessibility contrast. The primary action button is a welcoming, trustworthy blue (#007bff) to draw attention without creating anxiety. Elements are sized for clarity, with a larger heading font (18px) and comfortable body text (15px). These choices foster user trust and create a feeling of control, avoiding the high-pressure aesthetic of aggressive marketing pop-ups. The design prioritizes clarity and respect for the user's attention.


    Question 2: What triggers the appearance of this widget?

    The appearance of this widget is triggered by user engagement, not by an immediate, disruptive event. It would appear for new or non-logged-in users only after they have demonstrated interest in the site. For example, it might slide into view after the user has scrolled 60% down a product page or after they have navigated to their third page within the site. 
    
    This trigger is chosen to respect the user's initial browsing experience and autonomy. It avoids interrupting them the moment they arrive, which can be jarring and lead to immediate dismissal. By waiting for a sign of engagement, the offer feels more like a relevant invitation and less like an intrusive advertisement, thus preserving a sense of calm and control for the user. It will not appear during critical tasks like the checkout process to prevent distraction and protect the user's economic interests.
