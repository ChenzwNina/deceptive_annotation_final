Question 1: Can you describe how the widget looks, such as its colors, size of elements, and layout? Why do you make these design choices?

The widget is a clean, rectangular banner positioned horizontally near the top of the screen. It features a soft, light blue (#f0f8ff) background with a subtle border, creating a calm and trustworthy appearance. The layout is organized using flexbox, with an icon on the far left, followed by the main text, an email input field, and a call-to-action button. A clear 'X' for closing is on the far right. The heading uses a strong, dark blue for emphasis, while body text is a highly readable dark gray. The "Join for Free" button is a solid, vibrant blue to draw attention as the primary action. The design choices prioritize clarity and a non-intrusive feel. The friendly colors and generous spacing are intended to make the offer feel like a helpful invitation rather than an aggressive ad, encouraging user engagement without causing annoyance.

Question 2: What triggers the appearance of this widget?

The appearance of this widget should be triggered by user engagement, specifically for visitors who are not logged in or identified as members. A good trigger would be a time-and-scroll combination. For instance, the banner could slide down after the user has been on the page for 7-10 seconds OR after they have scrolled down 25% of the page. This approach ensures the user has had a moment to engage with the site's content and show interest before being presented with the membership offer. Triggering it immediately upon page load can be jarring and lead to "banner blindness" or immediate dismissal. This delayed and conditional trigger respects the user's initial browsing experience and presents the offer at a more opportune moment.
